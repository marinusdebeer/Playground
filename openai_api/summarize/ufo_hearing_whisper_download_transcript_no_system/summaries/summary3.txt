During a subcommittee hearing on unidentified anomalous phenomena or UAPs, the lack of transparency and accountability in the government's handling of UAP incidents is highlighted. The chair expresses concerns about the erosion of public trust and mentions prominent figures, such as Presidents Gerald Ford and Jimmy Carter, who have reported UAP sightings. The All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Officer (AARO) and the National Security Agency (NASSA) were established to coordinate efforts in detecting, identifying, and investigating UAPs, but their lack of transparency and accountability is concerning. The chair emphasizes the need for government transparency and accountability in UAP-related matters and calls for improved reporting for both the military and civilians. The chair expresses gratitude to the witnesses for their participation and dedication to safeguarding the American people's interests.

Representative Burchard from Tennessee emphasizes that the UAP issue is nonpartisan and thanks his colleagues for their support. He acknowledges the difficulties in organizing the hearing and calls for government transparency and trust. He states that they will focus on facts and uncover any cover-ups.

Representative Luna from Florida acknowledges the captivation of UAP sightings for decades and highlights the lack of transparency and failure to release explanations, footage, and classified documents by the government, leading to public skepticism. She emphasizes the need for an open-minded discussion and understanding the magnitude of UAPs. She also mentions concerns raised by service members who fear retribution for reporting UAP sightings.

Representative Garcia, the ranking member of the subcommittee, thanks the witnesses for their service and bravery in sharing their experiences. He acknowledges the importance of their testimonies and expresses gratitude to Congressman Burchard for bringing attention to the issue.

The hearing focuses on the lack of transparency and accountability in UAP-related matters and the need for government transparency. The witnesses share their experiences and provide compelling evidence. The members of the subcommittee show a strong interest in understanding and addressing the UAP phenomenon. The need for a secure reporting system for Pilots, reducing stigma, and making information available to the public is highlighted. The importance of a centralized location for processing UAP information and establishing a two-way communication loop is emphasized. Concerns are raised about the lack of reporting and transparency in both the military and commercial aviation sectors. The potential threat of UAPs to national security is addressed, highlighting the superior technology observed and the need to understand and respond to UAP encounters. The witnesses call for a system that encourages and facilitates civilian reporting. Mr. Garcia expresses his support and willingness to work on legislation related to UAP reporting. Mr. Gresch believes that the government possesses information about UAPs based on interviews with witnesses. The importance of answering these questions and finding answers is emphasized, and further collaboration on UAP reporting legislation is discussed.
