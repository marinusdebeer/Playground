The witnesses, including Lieutenant Ryan Graves, David Grush, and retired Navy Commander David Fravor, discuss the need for a system where pilots can report UAPs without fear of professional repercussions. They emphasize the lack of a proper reporting system and the dearth of data due to limited reporting in the past. The witnesses also address concerns about national security and the potential threat posed by UAPs. They highlight the need for a centralized location to gather and investigate UAP reports and stress the importance of transparency, accountability, and public trust. The witnesses describe the maneuvers and behaviors of UAPs that are outside the scope of current technology and our understanding. They believe that the government is in possession of UAPs based on interviews with witnesses. Representatives express their support for a reporting system and discuss the importance of civilian reporting and involvement in understanding UAP encounters.
