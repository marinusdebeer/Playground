During a subcommittee hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), witnesses including retired Navy Commander David Fravor, Lieutenant Ryan Graves, and David Grush, discussed the need for transparency and a reporting system for UAP encounters. They highlighted the lack of data and centralized location for investigating UAP reports, and expressed concerns about national security and potential threats posed by UAPs. Witnesses described UAP maneuvers and behaviors that defied current technology and understanding, and believed that the government possessed UAPs based on witness interviews. The witnesses also mentioned instances of retaliation and reprisals for coming forward with their testimonies. Representatives stressed the importance of government transparency and civilian involvement in understanding UAP encounters.
