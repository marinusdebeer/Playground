During the hearing on UAPs, witnesses discussed the need for a reporting system and centralized investigation for encounters with unidentified phenomena. They expressed concerns about potential threats to national security and described maneuvers that exceeded current technological capabilities. Witnesses also mentioned facing retaliation for speaking out. Representatives emphasized government transparency and civilian involvement in understanding UAP encounters. The discussions included classification guidelines, the need for more transparency with the public, and experiences with reporting UAP incidents. Witnesses touched on topics such as interdimensional phenomena and UAPs disabling sensor systems. The importance of changing the reporting culture and providing secure facilities for sensitive information was also highlighted.
